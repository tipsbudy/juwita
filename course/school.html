Sentence Levels in English

  • Non-Clause: a group of words without a subject and a verb.
    e.g. Playing in the school yard.
  • Simple Sentence: one subject + one verb.
    e.g. Students study English.
  • Compound Sentence: two simple sentences joined by a coordinating conjunction.
    e.g. Students study, and teachers teach.
  • Complex Sentence: one independent clause with one or more dependent clauses.
    e.g. Students study because exams are coming.
  • Compound-Complex Sentence: at least two independent clauses with one or more dependent clauses.
    e.g. Students study, and teachers teach because they want good results.
